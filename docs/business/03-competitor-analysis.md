# PitchUp — Competitor Analysis

## Competitive Landscape for UK Mobile Food Vendor Platforms

---

## 1. Doing Nothing (Facebook + Phone)

**What most vendors do today.** Post on Facebook the night before, take phone calls, hope for the best.

| Factor | Facebook/Phone | PitchUp |
|--------|---------------|---------|
| Cost | Free | Free tier available |
| Online ordering | ❌ | ✅ Pre-ordering with pickup codes |
| Fleet management | ❌ (WhatsApp groups) | ✅ Multi-van with individual KDS |
| Customer data | ❌ | ✅ Order history, analytics |
| Professional presence | ❌ Facebook page | ✅ Branded website |
| Discoverability | Algorithm-dependent | ✅ Live map, search |
| Scheduling | Manual posts | ✅ Route scheduling with time windows |
| Scalability | Breaks at 2+ vans | ✅ Unlimited vehicles |

**PitchUp advantage:** Everything they're doing manually, automated. Free tier means zero risk to try.

---

## 2. Deliveroo / UberEats / Just Eat

**The aggregator model.** High visibility but brutal economics and zero brand ownership.

| Factor | Deliveroo/UberEats | PitchUp |
|--------|-------------------|---------|
| Commission | 25-35% per order | **0%** |
| Brand visibility | Buried in listings | ✅ Vendor-branded site |
| Customer ownership | ❌ Platform owns the customer | ✅ Vendor owns the relationship |
| Multi-van support | ❌ Single location | ✅ Fleet management |
| Mobile food focus | ❌ Restaurant-first | ✅ Purpose-built |
| Payment model | Upfront online payment | Pay at van (lower friction) |
| Minimum order | Often required | No minimum |
| Live location | ❌ | ✅ Real-time map |
| Monthly cost | Variable (commission) | Fixed subscription |

**PitchUp advantage:** A chippy van doing £2,000/week on Deliveroo loses £500-700 to commission. PitchUp costs £29-99/mo flat. The maths is brutal — in PitchUp's favour.

**Example:** £2,000/week revenue × 30% commission = **£600/week to Deliveroo** = £2,400/month vs **£29/month on PitchUp Starter**.

| Factor | Square Online | PitchUp |
|--------|--------------|---------|

| Multi-van fleet | ❌ Single location | ✅ |
| Route scheduling | ❌ | ✅ |
| Live map | ❌ | ✅ |
| KDS | ❌ (separate product) | ✅ Built-in |
| Events system | ❌ | ✅ |
| Payment processing | Square payments (fees apply) | Vendor's own (at van) |
| Branding | Limited templates | ✅ Food-vendor templates |
| Free tier | ✅ (limited) | ✅ |

**PitchUp advantage:** Square works for a fixed café. It doesn't understand routes, live locations, or managing 4 vans across 22 stops. PitchUp does.

| Factor | Flipdish | PitchUp |
|--------|----------|---------|
| Target market | Restaurants, takeaways | Mobile food vendors |
| Commission | Custom (often % based) | £0 commission |
| Multi-van fleet | ❌ | ✅ |
| Route scheduling | ❌ | ✅ |
| Live location tracking | ❌ | ✅ |
| Minimum contract | Often annual + setup fee | Month-to-month, free tier |
| Self-service setup | ❌ Sales-led | ✅ Self-serve in minutes |
| Events | ❌ | ✅ |
| Price | Typically £200+/mo | From £0/mo |

**PitchUp advantage:** Flipdish is enterprise restaurant tech. Overkill and overpriced for a burger van. PitchUp is right-sized and right-priced.

**PitchUp is the only platform purpose-built for mobile food vendors that combines fleet management, live location tracking, pre-ordering, and vendor-branded websites — with zero commission.**

Every competitor is either built for restaurants (wrong use case), takes commission (wrong economics), or is an aggregator (wrong brand model). PitchUp is none of those things.
