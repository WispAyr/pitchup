## Demo Script

### Pre-Demo (2 minutes)

*"Thanks for taking the time, [Name]. Before I show you anything, can I ask — how do you let your customers know where you'll be? And do you get many pre-orders or is it mostly walk-up?"*

**Listen.** Their pain points will tell you which features to emphasise.

Common answers:
- "Facebook" → Emphasise branded website, professionalism
- "Phone/text" → Emphasise online ordering, automation
- "We don't take pre-orders" → Emphasise the pre-order flow, reduced waste
- "We have multiple vans" → Emphasise fleet management, per-van KDS

### Demo Flow (10 minutes)

**1. Customer Experience (3 mins)**
- Show a live vendor site (Joanna's)
- Walk through: find van on map → browse menu → place order → get pickup code
- *"This is what YOUR customers would see, with YOUR brand, YOUR colours."*

**2. Vendor Dashboard (3 mins)**
- Show the KDS: orders appearing, audio alert, marking complete
- Show route scheduling: adding stops, time windows
- Go Live button: *"One tap and you're on the map."*

**3. Fleet Management (2 mins)** *(if multi-van)*
- Show adding vehicles
- Each van has own schedule, own KDS
- *"Your driver at [location] sees their orders. Your driver at [other location] sees theirs."*

**4. The Extras (2 mins)**
- Vouchers: *"Run a deal for quiet Tuesday? Done."*
- Events: *"Got a festival this weekend? Register and take pre-orders from event-goers."*
- Analytics: *"Know what sells where, and when."*

### Close

*"The free plan gives you one vehicle with the basics. If you want pre-ordering, KDS, and scheduling, that's the Starter at £29/month. Want to give the free version a go and see how it feels?"*

**Always start them on free.** Let the product sell the upgrade.

## Objection Handling

### "I just use Facebook, it works fine"

*"Totally get it — Facebook's where your customers are. PitchUp isn't replacing Facebook. It's giving you somewhere to send them. You post 'We're at [location] today' — but now you add a link where they can actually order. You still post, but now orders come in before you arrive."*

### "My customers prefer to just come up to the van"

*"That's great, and they still can. Pre-ordering doesn't replace walk-ups — it adds to them. Some customers can't wait in a queue (lunch break, picking up kids). Now they can order at 11am, pick up at 12, no queue. You're not losing walk-ups, you're gaining the ones who'd have gone elsewhere."*

### "I can't afford another monthly cost"

*"The free plan is genuinely free — one vehicle, basic menu and orders, no time limit. Use it for a month and see if it brings in extra orders. If it doesn't, you've lost nothing. If it brings in even 3-4 extra orders a day, the Starter plan at £29/month pays for itself in a single shift."*

### "I don't want to deal with online payments"

*"You don't have to. PitchUp doesn't process payments. Customers order, get a pickup code, and pay you at the van — cash, card, however you normally take payment. Nothing changes except they've ordered ahead."*

### "I'm not very tech-savvy"

*"The whole thing takes about 10 minutes to set up. Pick your colours, add your menu, set your schedule. If you can post on Facebook, you can use PitchUp. And if you get stuck, we'll set it up for you — free."*

### "What's the catch? Why is the free tier free?"

*"No catch. Free gets you one vehicle with the basics. When you want pre-ordering, a kitchen display, scheduling, or more vehicles — that's the paid plans. We're betting that once you use it, you'll want the extras. If you don't, the free plan stays free forever."*

### "I already use Deliveroo/UberEats"

*"You can keep using them — PitchUp isn't a replacement for aggregator traffic. But think about this: every order on Deliveroo costs you 25-35%. On PitchUp, it costs you nothing. The customers who already follow you on Facebook, who already know your name — send them to your PitchUp site instead. Keep Deliveroo for new customer discovery, use PitchUp for your regulars."*

### "I only have one van, do I really need this?"

*"That's exactly what the free plan is for. One van, your own website, online ordering. When the business grows and you add a second van, you'll already have the platform. But even with one van — having your own website with ordering makes you look more professional than anyone else at the market."*
