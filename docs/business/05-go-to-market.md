# PitchUp — Go-To-Market Strategy

---

## Phase 1: Foundation (Months 1-3)

### Objective: 50 signups, 10 paying vendors

**1. Flagship Case Study**
- Document Joanna's Chippy Van success (4 vans, 22 locations)
- Before/after metrics: orders, efficiency, customer reach
- Video testimonial (phone-shot, authentic — not corporate)
- This becomes the anchor for ALL sales conversations

**2. Local Market Saturation (Ayrshire)**
- Visit every chippy van, burger truck, coffee cart in Ayrshire personally
- Offer free setup assistance (onboard them in person)
- Goal: 20 local vendors on free tier → 5 convert to paid
- Ayrshire becomes the proof-of-concept market

**3. Social Media Launch**
- Instagram + TikTok: behind-the-scenes of food vendors using PitchUp
- Facebook: food vendor groups (NCASS members, mobile catering forums)
- Content: "How [vendor name] went from Facebook posts to online ordering in 10 minutes"
- 3 posts/week minimum

**4. Website & SEO**
- Landing page with clear value prop, pricing, and Joanna's case study
- Blog: "How to start taking online orders from your food van" (SEO play)
- Target keywords: "food van online ordering", "mobile catering software", "chippy van website"

---

## Phase 2: Regional Expansion (Months 4-8)

### Objective: 200 signups, 50 paying vendors

**5. Event Presence**
- Attend 3-5 food truck festivals/markets as PitchUp (not selling food — selling to food sellers)
- NCASS events, Scottish Street Food Awards, regional food festivals
- Simple stand: iPad demo, flyers, QR code to free signup
- Budget: £500-1,000 per event

**6. Partnership Channel**
- **NCASS (National Caterers Association)** — member benefit partnership
- **Van conversion companies** — recommend PitchUp to every new van build
- **Event organisers** — "All vendors at this event use PitchUp for pre-orders"
- **Food suppliers** — cross-promotion with food wholesalers who serve mobile vendors

**7. Content Marketing**
- YouTube: "Day in the life" series following vendors who use PitchUp
- Blog: operational guides (food hygiene, route planning, menu pricing)
- Newsletter: weekly vendor tips + PitchUp feature highlights

**8. Referral Program**
- Existing vendors refer new vendors
- **Reward:** 1 month free on their current plan per successful referral (must convert to paid)
- **Referred vendor:** Extended 60-day free trial on Starter
- Simple, clear, no complicated points systems

---

## Phase 3: National Scale (Months 9-18)

### Objective: 500+ signups, 100+ paying vendors

**9. Paid Acquisition**
- Facebook/Instagram ads targeting food vendor business pages
- Google Ads on "food van ordering system", "mobile catering software"
- Budget: £1,000-2,000/mo (CAC target: £30-40)
- Retargeting: anyone who visited but didn't sign up

**10. PR & Media**
- Scottish startup story angle: "Solo founder builds platform for Scotland's chippy vans"
- Food industry trade press: The Caterer, Mobile Catering magazine
- Local press: Ayrshire success stories
- Tech press: bootstrapped SaaS, AI-augmented solo founder

**11. Event Organiser Platform**
- Offer event organisers a dashboard to manage all vendor pre-orders at their events
- This becomes a B2B2C channel: organiser signs up → all their vendors need PitchUp
- One event with 30 vendors = 30 new signups

**12. Geographic Clusters**
- Replicate the Ayrshire model in other regions
- Target: Glasgow, Edinburgh, Manchester, Birmingham, London
- Find one "Joanna's" in each city — local champion vendor

## Pricing Psychology

### Why the Free Tier Works

1. **Zero risk** — vendor tries it with nothing to lose
2. **Habit formation** — once they're using the KDS and getting orders, switching back to Facebook feels painful
3. **Natural upgrade trigger** — they buy a second van → need Starter. They want events → need Pro
4. **Social proof** — free vendors still appear on the map, making the platform look busier
5. **Data** — free users tell us what features matter before they'll pay

### Annual Plan Psychology
- 20% discount is meaningful for small business owners
- Upfront payment = commitment = lower churn
- "Save £70/year" on Starter is real money for a chippy van
